Darjeeling Tour

Tour help for Darjeeling along with my own personal experiences.

Darjeeling is a hill station in the Indian state of West Bengal. It is located in the Eastern Himalayas, at an average elevation of 6,709 feet (2,045 m). Darjeeling is famous for its tea plantations, which produce some of the finest tea in the world. The city is also known for its scenic beauty, with stunning views of the Himalayas, including Mount Kanchenjunga, the third highest mountain in the world.

Darjeeling was founded in the early 19th century by the British East India Company. The company was looking for a place to grow tea, and Darjeeling's climate and soil were ideal for tea cultivation. The first tea plantation in Darjeeling was established in 1856. Today, there are over 80 tea plantations in the Darjeeling district.

In addition to tea, Darjeeling is also known for its handicrafts, including textiles, woodcarvings, and jewelry. The city is also home to a number of schools and colleges, including the Government College of Darjeeling, which was founded in 1864.

Darjeeling is a popular tourist destination, and the city's economy is largely based on tourism. The peak tourist seasons are April to June and September to November.

Here are some additional facts about Darjeeling:

  • The name Darjeeling means "place of the thunderbolt" in Tibetan.
  • Darjeeling is known as the "Queen of the Hills".
  • The climate in Darjeeling is temperate, with average temperatures ranging from 10 to 25 degrees Celsius (50 to 77 degrees Fahrenheit).
  • The city receives an average annual rainfall of 120 inches (300 centimeters).
  • The official language of Darjeeling is Bengali, but English is also widely spoken.
  • The population of Darjeeling is about 120,000 people.

Tour lists

  1. Can either fly to IXB, or cath a train or a bus to NJP.
  2. Halt one night in NJP or IXB.
  3. Book a cab and set off for Darjeeling early on the next day(to have sometime to visit Mirik, Lepchajagat and Nepal-India border on the way).
  4. Reside in an already booked hotel (online or offline).
  5. Visit Lamahatta Eco park, Peshok tea garden, Rock Garden and return to hotel by sundown.
  6. On the next day visit Gopal Dhara Tea Garden, Tribeni, Teesta river and return to hotel by sundown.
  7. Last day visit the Tiger Hill, Batasia Loop, Local Market in the morning and Kalimpong in the afternoon followed by hotel before dark.
  8. On the return journey you can either fly from IXB airport or can take a train or bus on the next day. We were to catch a flight from IXB to CCU but due our flight being cancelled, we had to return by a car.
